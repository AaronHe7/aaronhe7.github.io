Mathematics

Mathematics is an extremely broad field, which generally includes computation, logical thinking, and pattern recognition. Mathematics has no single accepted definition. It plays a major role in the world, as it allows us to create predictions about the future, and make judgements based on logic and previous knowledge. When many people think about math, they think of numbers, heavy computation, and the memorization of many formulas, although it is much more than just that.
What makes mathematics an interesting field are the difficult problems that are solvable, and the problems that have yet to be solved. Difficult problems that are solvable highlights what is currently possible with modern mathematical knowledge, and unsolved problems shows how there is still much to be discovered. The International Mathematical Olympiad and the Putnam Competition are two examples of math competitions that have extremely difficult solvable problems, which require problem-solving skills and years of training. The Millennium Problems are examples of unsolved problems, which offers a prize of one million dollars for a solution to any of the problems. Ultimately, mathematics can, at the same time, show how much we understand about the world, and how little we understand as well.

Computer Science

Despite the word "science" appearing inside the name, I would argue that computer science is more of a mathematics than a science. Computer science is mainly the study of algorithms and how it is used to interact with data. Since the creation of algorithms requires problem-solving, and logical reasoning, the same skills required for discoveries in math. However, once an algorithm is written, how does one know, with total certainty that the algorithm is correct? Sure, they could test the algorithm with many test cases and assume it is correct if it passes each test case, but to be completely sure, one would write a formal proof to justify its correctness. Science, on the other hand uses the scientific method; it assumes a theory correct after it passes many tests. This is not to say that the scientific theory is inferior, as a mathematical proof does not suit the idea of discovering properties of the universe. For me, the most interesting aspect of computer science is creating projects and solving difficult problems. These require you to think outside the box and actively push yourself to increase your knowledge.
